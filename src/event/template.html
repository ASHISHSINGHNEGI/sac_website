GOONJ 2023

THE ANNUAL CULTURAL FEST

GOVIND BALLADH PANT INSTITUTE OF ENGINEERING & TECHNOLOGY, PAURI GHURDAURI – 246194 (UTTARAKHAND) (An Autonomous Institute of Government of Uttarakhand)

About

Goonj is the flagship cultural event of GB Pant Institute. It brings together students, faculty, and staff to celebrate the vibrant and diverse cultural heritage of our college. The event showcases various art forms, performances, competitions, and exhibitions.

Through Goonj, we aim to provide a platform for students to express their talents, celebrate creativity, and foster a sense of community. Join us for an unforgettable experience filled with music, dance, drama, and much more!

Day Zero

Rally

  • Duration: 1 hour
  • Starting Time: 2:00 PM
  • About: Theme-based event (open theme)
  • Topics: Sanskrit, demonetization, corruption, human rights, women in education, and employment
  • Details: The rally will be conducted from the administration building to the Applied Science Department, concluding at the auditorium ground. Points will be awarded based on discipline, collaboration, and theme presentation.
  • Point Structure: 1st Place: 100 points, 2nd Place: 80 points, 3rd Place: 60 points

Nukkad

  • Duration: 8–12 minutes per house
  • Venue: Auditorium
  • Starting Time: 3:00 PM
  • About: It is an open theme event (theme will be chosen by houses themselves)
  • Rules and Guidelines: 8-15 participants are allowed. The time duration is limited to 8–10 minutes. Participants can use props if needed. Acts should not violate any religion, community, caste, or other social or cultural sensitivity aspect. The use of any abusive or obscene language is prohibited.
  • Point Structure: 1st Place: 100 points, 2nd Place: 80 points, 3rd Place: 60 points

Filmistan

  • Starting Time: 5:00 PM
  • Rules and Guidelines:
  • 1. Only one entry per team is allowed.
  • 2. The time limit of the film should be 5–15 minutes (including titles, credits, and bloopers).
  • 3. Any GBPIET family member can be a part of the film. The students of the same houses are allowed to act or have a dialogue in the film. However, the presence of other house students is not a reason for disqualification (other house members cannot act in the movie).
  • 4. The film should be submitted in H.264 codec MP4 format with a minimum resolution of 1280x720 pixels. However, 1920 x 1080p is recommended.
  • 5. All houses must submit their movie genre and theme to the SAC before 23:59:59 on May 12, 2023.
  • 6. Accepted genres are horror, comedy, experimental, psychological thriller, thriller, musical, action, sci-fi, western, and drama.
  • 7. The film should have good audio quality and be understandable to a general audience.
  • 8. Use of stock footage is allowed. However, they should be royalty-free, and the production team should have their rights.
  • 9. Stock footage should have a maximum screen time of 30% of the total film length.
  • 10. The languages to be used are Hindi and English. In the case of implementing other languages, subtitles are necessary.
  • 11. No abusive language, vulgarity, or cruelty is allowed.
  • 12. The movie should be submitted on or before May 16, 2023, at 11:59 PM.
  • 13. All the shots should be taken from the Phone (mobile cinematography) in 4k 30fps.

Judging Criteria

  • 1. Theme: How well the film aligns with the chosen genre and effectively conveys the intended message or story.
  • 2. Camera Work: Evaluation of the cinematography, framing, composition, camera movement, and overall visual aesthetics of the film.
  • 3. Video-Audio Clarity: The quality and clarity of both the video and audio elements, ensuring that the audience can clearly perceive the content without distractions or technical issues.
  • 4. Acting: Assessment of the performances by the actors, including their portrayal of characters, delivery of dialogues, emotional range, and overall believability.
  • 5. Story: Analysis of the plot structure, character development, pacing, narrative coherence, and the overall impact and engagement of the storytelling.

Points Structure

Day One

Inauguration of the Event

JUST DANCE

Guidelines:
  • 2 entries from each team can be both boys and girls or 1 boy and 1 girl. (It is a duet dance competition)
  • Participants have 3-5 minutes for their performance.
  • The use of props is allowed.
  • Projector is not allowed.
  • Point Structure:
    • 1st Place: 50 points
    • 2nd Place:30 points
    • 3rd Place: 20 points

TAMASHA

Guidelines:
  • Scene changes should have narration.
  • 8-15 participants are allowed in the team.
  • The time duration is limited to 8–15 minutes.
  • Participants can use props if they need to.
  • Vulgarity isn't allowed.
  • Criteria for judging:
    • Acting
    • Stage usage
    • Theme/Script
    • Dialogue delivery
    • Makeup/Costume
  • Point Structure: Point card system
    • 1st Place: 100 points
    • 2nd Place:75 points
    • 3rd Place: 50 points

FOOTLOOSE

Guidelines:
  • Only one entry per team is allowed.
  • A minimum of six members should be participating per team. There are no maximum limits.
  • No member should leave the stage during a performance.
  • Formation is mandatory.
  • Each team has 6-8 minutes for its performance.
  • Criteria for judging:
    • Coordination
    • Formation
    • Dancing
  • Point Structure: Point card system
    • 1st Place: 100 points
    • 2nd Place:80 points
    • 3rd Place: 60 points

FOLK EVENT GROUP DANCE, SOLO DANCE

Guidelines:
  • only folk songs, no Bollywood music.
  • Each team has 4-6 minutes for performance.
  • Number of participants are limited to 5 - 12 per team for group performance.
  • Participants are not allowed to leave the stage until the performance is finished.
  • Dress should be according to the folk form selected.
  • Criteria for judging:
    • Coordination
    • Expression.
    • Dancing
    • Dressing.
