Judging Criteria

  • 1. Theme: How well the film aligns with the chosen genre and effectively conveys the intended message or story.
  • 2. Camera Work: Evaluation of the cinematography, framing, composition, camera movement, and overall visual aesthetics of the film.
  • 3. Video-Audio Clarity: The quality and clarity of both the video and audio elements, ensuring that the audience can clearly perceive the content without distractions or technical issues.
  • 4. Acting: Assessment of the performances by the actors, including their portrayal of characters, delivery of dialogues, emotional range, and overall believability.
  • 5. Story: Analysis of the plot structure, character development, pacing, narrative coherence, and the overall impact and engagement of the storytelling.
